## LinkedIn — FAA Part 450 timeline

The FAA Part 450 statutory clock is 180 days from a complete application. The reality is anywhere from 6 months to 18 months from kickoff to a license, with the variance driven almost entirely by pre-application consultation under 14 CFR 413.5.

We just published a timeline-focused explainer with concrete per-operator durations: Astra issued February 4, 2022 after roughly 8 months; Blue Origin New Glenn 114 days post-acceptance, December 27, 2024; Starship VOL 23-129 April 14, 2023 (six days before IFT-1); plus what the 2025 SpARC + Executive Order 14335 do to the timeline going forward.

Useful before a kickoff meeting with FAA AST.

## LinkedIn — NRC pre-application engagement

Pre-application engagement is not required by 10 CFR Part 50, 52, or 53. It is, however, the single largest predictor of how long the docketed review actually takes.

Kairos Hermes 2 received a Construction Permit 16 months after docketing in November 2024. The NRC published an 18-month review schedule for the X-energy Xe-100 at Long Mott citing continuous engagement with X-energy since September 2018.

We published a guide to the formal flow under DANU-ISG-2022-01, the Regulatory Engagement Plan concept from NEI 18-06, LIC-500 Revision 9 topical reports, and the ARCAP and TICAP frameworks. Real dockets, real durations.

## LinkedIn — FCC 5-year deorbit rule

The FCC Second Report and Order at FCC 22-74 shortened the post-mission disposal benchmark for LEO satellites from 25 years to 5. It is codified at 47 CFR 25.283(e) and operative for applications filed on or after September 29, 2024.

Almost every commercial Part 25 application now needs an updated end-of-mission disposal analysis with a probability of successful disposal of at least 0.9 and a maximum probability of collision with large objects of 0.001.

We published a complete explainer including the 2,000 km LEO boundary, the GEO/MEO "passing through" clause, the grandfathering window, and the alternative compliance path for non-US-licensed operators with their own regulatory oversight.

Link: https://invariant-ai.com/fcc-5-year-deorbit-rule

## LinkedIn — NOAA remote-sensing license tiers

The NOAA Commercial Remote Sensing Regulatory Affairs (CRSRA) office sorts every US private Earth-observation system into Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 under 15 CFR 960.6. The trigger is whether the system can collect unenhanced data "substantially the same" as what is already available from foreign or domestic operators.

Twenty-three systems held Tier 3 licenses as of Q1 2023. NOAA eliminated 39 Tier 3 temporary conditions on July 19, 2023 after foreign capability matured in SAR, nighttime imaging, and shortwave infrared.

We published a complete guide to the three tiers, the 60-day shot clock under 15 CFR 960.7(c), the cybersecurity floor (NIST-approved 256-bit encryption), and the resolved imaging rule for artificial resident space objects.

Link: https://invariant-ai.com/noaa-remote-sensing-license-tiers

## LinkedIn — IN-SPACe authorisation for India

If you are operating a satellite from India, the regulatory architecture is IN-SPACe + ISRO + DOS + WPC, governed by the Indian Space Policy 2023 and the Norms, Guidelines, and Procedures notified on May 3, 2024.

The activity is sorted into six categories. The clock is 75 to 120 days from a complete application. Foreign-direct-investment rules were rewritten by DPIIT Press Note 1 of 2024 on March 4, 2024. ITU coordination flows through the Wireless Planning and Coordination Wing under the Department of Telecommunications.

We published a complete guide to the process, with the application flow step by step.

Link: https://invariant-ai.com/space-compliance-india

## LinkedIn — Nuclear compliance in India (SHANTI Act)

India's nuclear compliance regime is being rewritten. The SHANTI Act 2025 received presidential assent on December 20, 2025, repealing both the Atomic Energy Act 1962 and the Civil Liability for Nuclear Damage Act 2010. It opens reactor licensing to Indian-incorporated private companies and removes the supplier right-of-recourse under Section 17(b) that stalled Westinghouse, GE-Hitachi, and EDF deals at Jaitapur and Kovvada for over a decade.

We published a full guide to the new architecture including AERB's role under the SHANTI framework, the tiered operator liability structure, and what foreign vendors need to do differently.

Link: https://invariant-ai.com/nuclear-compliance-india

## LinkedIn — advanced reactor licensing in the EU

There is no single EU nuclear regulator. Reactor licensing remains a Member State competence under the Euratom Treaty. So advanced reactor developers navigate UK ONR (Generic Design Assessment, four steps, typical 4-year duration), France ASNR (formed January 1, 2025 from ASN + IRSN), Finland STUK, Romania CNCAN, Czech SUJB, Sweden SSM, Spain CSN, Hungary HAEA.

We published a regulator-by-regulator guide with real project tracking: Rolls-Royce SMR (Step 2 completed August 2024), GE-Hitachi BWRX-300 (Step 1 commenced July 18, 2024), Holtec SMR-300 (Step 1 commenced 2024), EDF EPR2 at Penly 1-2 (ASNR expertise phase complete).

Useful before a strategic review.
